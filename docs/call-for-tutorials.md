# Call for Tutorials

ICPP 2026 invites proposals for tutorials to be held on the first day of the conference (28 September 2026). Tutorials provide an excellent opportunity to share expertise, educate the community, and engage with a global audience of researchers, developers, and practitioners in parallel computing and HPC.

We particularly encourage proposals that address emerging trends, current challenges, and cutting-edge technologies in parallel computing. Tutorials that explore forward-looking topics and reflect the evolving landscape of the field are especially welcome.

## Proposal Format

Tutorial proposals should include the following information:

1. **Title**: Provide a concise and descriptive title for your tutorial.

2. **Abstract**: Include a summary (maximum 300 words) that clearly describes the tutorial's focus, objectives, and key takeaways. The abstract should be written to attract attendees with varying levels of expertise.

3. **Presenter Information**
    - List all presenters, including:
        - Full name
        - Title / position
        - Affiliation (university, company, or organization)
        - Contact email

4. **Target Audience**
    - Specify the intended audience (e.g., beginner, intermediate, advanced) and any prerequisites or required background knowledge.

5. **Tutorial Structure**
    - **Duration**: Indicate whether the tutorial is intended to be a half-day (3-4 hours) or full-day (6-8 hours) session.
    - **Outline**: Provide an outline of the tutorial content, including topics covered, estimated time allocation for each section, and any hands-on activities or demonstrations.
    - **Learning objectives**: Clearly define what attendees will learn and be able to do after participating in the tutorial.
    - **Hands-on activities (Optional)**: If applicable, describe any interactive or hands-on components of the tutorial and how they will enhance the learning experience.

6. **Past Experience (Optional)**: If you have conducted similar tutorials at past conferences or events, please provide details, including feedback or evaluations if available.

## Proposal Submission

Please submit your proposal as a single PDF file through the ICPP submission system ([https://ssl.linklings.net/conferences/icpp](https://ssl.linklings.net/conferences/icpp){:target="_blank"}) by the specified deadline.

## Tutorial Facility

ICPP 2026 will provide one room for each accepted tutorial. Tutorials may be scheduled as either half-day or full-day sessions. Each room will be equipped with a projector and screen.

## Fees and Registration

ICPP 2026 conference registrants may attend tutorials at no additional cost. Therefore, there will be no "tutorial-only" registration option. No financial support (e.g., registration fee waivers or travel funding) will be provided for tutorial organizers.
